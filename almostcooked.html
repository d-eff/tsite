Almost Cooked

Interface Design, User Research and Brand Identity for a recipe website and iOS app.

Interface Design

Initially we were presented with the challenge of creating a recipe search engine with features such as shopping lists, meal planning, and coupon integration. Many sites existed with similar functionality, but few were gaining any traction. Instead of duplicating products that already existed, we were determined to ask, “Why?”.

Try the interactive prototype here.

The app was meant to work as a companion to the website. It incorporates many aspects of the mobile site while following iOS7 design best practices.

User Research

We began with an intensive period of consumer research, studying competitor sites and reaching out to our user demographic. I conducted a series of phone interviews, and through commonalities created personas that fairly represented our target users.

I named our primary persona and gave her a backstory to promote empathy in our conversations about user experience. Joyce was the center of every decision about user flow, visual design, and even tone of voice.

Our secondary persona, Kim, also represents a large portion of users. Having a second persona helped us cater to an even larger audience.

I created a storyboard to get a clearer sense of where we could enhance Joyce’s experience. Using narrative gave context to our user flow.

Brand Identity

While creating mood boards for the project I drew inspiration from objects one might find in Joyce’s kitchen. This style tile was based on vintage Pyrex bowls and includes the illustration style of 1950’s cookbooks. The design is bright and modern, yet familiar to Joyce.
