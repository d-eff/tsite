Pet

A piece of fur that can detect petting. And it purrs.

The Process

This project started as a material exploration for developing soft interfaces. During my research I had students interact with a variety of soft surfaces. I found that everyone reacted imediately to fur, petting along its grain. The idea behind Pet was to be able to sense and provide feedback for that gesture.

I started by creating a simple pressure sensor using pressure-sensitive fabric. In my first prototypes petting the fur altered the brightness of an LED.

In order to detect petting I needed to detect both pressure and motion. I programmed an Arduino microcontroller to compare pressure across the fur and trigger a vibration motor.

The biggest challenge was to differentiate directions. I used just two inputs I added LEDs to my circuit to provide additional feedback while I iterated the code. Because the fabric was so sensitive I added a rubber base to absorb vibrations from other sources. I chose a flat, flexible design to emphasize the simplicity of the circuit.

Pet was featured in the ITP 2014 Winter Show.
